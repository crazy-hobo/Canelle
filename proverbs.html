Cannelle la Demoiselle

French Proverbs Page 1 * "Being red with anger" means: It means getting carried away, being very angry. * To "pick up the moon" means: It means being able to do everything for someone or something. Page 2 * To "make the 400 moves" means: It means doing silly things. In connection with the 400 cannon shots in Montauban too. Page 3 * To "give your tongue to the cat" means: This expression is well known for saying that we do not know the answer. 4 (2) * To “sing like a pan” means: It means to sing badly. * “Tighten like sardines” means: It means being very tight, very close to each other. 5 (1) * To “wait 107 years” means: The expression means a very long waiting time. Notre Dame de Paris would have been built in 107 years. (Actually even more: about 200 years.) 6 (1) "See you soon" * “To one of these four” means: This expression means a short duration. Shortcut for "See you soon"! 7 (1) * "Crack the Crust" means: This expression means having a quick and simple snack. 8 (2) * "In a row of onions" means: Arrange on the same line, one behind the other, and well organized. * "Wise as an image" means: Without moving, calm and well educated. 9 none 10 (4) * "Like your pocket" means: Know a place, a city, a country very well, etc. * “Oh puree!” Means: Oh la la! It is an exclamation to express stupor, astonishment. * "Raining rain" means: It means that it is raining very hard and suddenly. * "Take Escapette Powder" means: It means fleeing or leaving quickly. 11 (3) * "Being in the moon" means: It means dreaming or not hearing. * "Staying in check" means: Be wise, no nonsense! The expression alludes to the code of card games and the symbolism of these emblems. The tile would be the projectile launched by crossbow. So you have to have a stable position to reach your goal. * "Under the nose" means: It means that you don't have something, you miss something. 12 (1) * “Staying mouth bee” means: It means keeping your mouth wide open in surprise and astonishment. 13 (1) * "Put yourself in your head" means: It means being preoccupied and reminiscent of hammer blows that would ring in the head. The hammer is a old form of hammer.
